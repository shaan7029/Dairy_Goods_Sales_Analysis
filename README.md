# Dairy_Goods_Sales_Analysis
## Project Overview

This project explores the dairy goods market in India using a comprehensive dataset covering **dairy farms, products, brands, storage conditions, and sales channels** from 2019 to 2022. The Power BI dashboard provides multi-page interactive visualizations to support data-driven decisions in the dairy industry.

---

## Key Insights

- **Delhi and Chandigarh** have the highest total land area and cow population among all locations, but **Tamil Nadu and West Bengal** generate the highest revenue per cow per acre, indicating better productivity efficiency.
- **Curd (12.4%), Lassi (10.4%), and Buttermilk (10.2%)** are the top 3 dairy products by availability volume across storage conditions.
- Products with **weekly shelf life** dominate stock quantities (643,767 units), far exceeding monthly (232,153) and daily (146,548) shelf-life products.
- **Ambient storage** provides the longest average shelf life (106 days), making it the most suitable for long-distance distribution.
- **Ghee** has the highest average expiry span (106 days), while **Curd** has the lowest (6 days), making inventory timing critical for fresh products.
- Sales are **evenly distributed** across Retail, Wholesale, and Online channels, suggesting a mature multi-channel distribution ecosystem.
- The decomposition tree reveals **Yogurt** as the most frequently occurring product in West Bengal's Amul brand transactions.
